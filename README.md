# 🏥 Hospital Operations Dashboard | Power BI

An interactive **Hospital Operations Dashboard** developed in **Microsoft Power BI** to monitor hospital performance, patient outcomes, financial metrics, and operational efficiency. The dashboard transforms raw hospital data into actionable insights that support evidence-based decision-making for healthcare administrators.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Healthcare organizations generate large volumes of operational data daily. Without effective visualization, it becomes difficult to identify trends, monitor performance, and make informed decisions.

This project presents a two-page interactive Power BI dashboard that provides a comprehensive overview of hospital operations, including patient admissions, disease trends, ward performance, insurance coverage, and financial performance.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

The dashboard was designed to answer the following business questions:

- How many patients were admitted during the reporting period?
- What is the average hospital bill?
- What is the average patient length of stay?
- Which wards handle the highest patient volume?
- Which diagnoses occur most frequently?
- Which diagnoses generate the most revenue?
- How are hospital bills distributed across insurance providers?
- How do admissions vary throughout the year?
- Which age groups account for the highest admissions?
- How does length of stay vary across wards and gender?

# 📌 Key Findings

## 1. Hospital Activity

- Total patient admissions reached **1,500**.
- The hospital operates across **9 wards**.
- The average patient bill is **$3,324**.
- Patients stay an average of **16 days** before discharge.

---

## 2. Patient Outcomes

| Outcome | Patients |
|----------|----------|
| Discharged | **1,095** |
| Referred | **208** |
| Deceased | **197** |

### Insight

Approximately **73%** of admitted patients were discharged successfully, indicating positive clinical outcomes.

---

## 3. Ward Performance

The **General Ward** recorded the highest patient volume (**316 admissions**), making it the busiest department.

Other major contributors include:

- ICU
- Cardiology
- Orthopaedics
- Oncology
- Emergency
- Paediatrics

---

## 4. Most Common Diagnoses

| Diagnosis | Frequency |
|------------|-----------|
| Typhoid | 123 |
| Malaria | 123 |
| Hypertension | 114 |
| Heart Failure | 85 |
| Measles | 84 |

### Insight

Typhoid and Malaria remain the leading causes of hospital admissions, highlighting the continued burden of infectious diseases.

---

## 5. Highest Revenue-Generating Diagnoses

The leading contributors to hospital revenue are:

- Hypertension
- Malaria
- Typhoid
- Heart Failure
- Measles

### Insight

Although Typhoid and Malaria account for the highest admission volumes, **Hypertension generates the highest revenue**, likely due to longer treatment duration, specialized investigations, and chronic disease management.

---

## 6. Insurance Coverage

Hospital bills are financed through multiple insurance providers.

| Insurance Provider | Contribution |
|-------------------|--------------|
| Corporate | 21.46% |
| NHIS | 20.20% |
| NHIS (Secondary) | 19.85% |
| HMO | 19.45% |
| Others | 19.03% |

### Insight

Corporate insurance contributes the largest share of hospital revenue, demonstrating strong reliance on employer-sponsored healthcare plans.

---

## 7. Monthly Admission Trend

Admissions remain relatively stable throughout the year with only moderate fluctuations.

Peak admission periods occur around:

- July
- September
- November

### Insight

The absence of extreme seasonal variation supports more predictable workforce and resource planning.

---

## 8. Average Bill by Ward

The **ICU** recorded the highest average patient bill.

Other high-cost departments include:

- Cardiology
- Oncology
- Surgery
- Emergency

### Insight

Critical care services naturally require more expensive treatments, specialized equipment, and intensive monitoring.

# 💡 Business Insights

The dashboard highlights several operational trends:

- The hospital maintains a strong discharge rate, with nearly three-quarters of patients successfully discharged.
- Infectious diseases continue to drive a significant proportion of admissions.
- Chronic diseases such as hypertension contribute disproportionately to hospital revenue.
- ICU services represent the highest-cost area of patient care.
- Corporate insurance remains the largest contributor to hospital billing.
- Admission volumes remain relatively stable throughout the year, enabling effective resource planning.

---

# 📈 Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

- Strengthen infectious disease prevention initiatives through public health education, improved sanitation, and vaccination campaigns.
- Expand chronic disease management programs, particularly for hypertension, to improve patient outcomes and optimize long-term care.
- Allocate additional staff and resources to the General Ward, which experiences the highest patient volume.
- Improve ICU resource utilization through early intervention strategies and optimized admission protocols.
- Strengthen partnerships with corporate insurance providers to sustain revenue growth.
- Conduct further investigation into mortality trends across wards to identify opportunities for clinical improvement.
- Implement predictive analytics using historical admission data to improve staffing, bed management, and resource allocation.
